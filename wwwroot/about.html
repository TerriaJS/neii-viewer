About the NEII Viewer

The National Environmental Information Infrastructure (NEII) Programme in collaboration with NICTA has developed the NEII Viewer. The NEII Viewer leverages TerriaJS to display NEII conformant data services. Data currently available through the Viewer includes:

  • the Marine Water Quality Dashboard, which provides water quality information essential to maintaining a vibrant and healthy reef ecosystem;
  • the Australian Hydrological Geospatial Fabric (Geofabric), is a specialised Geographic Information System (GIS). It registers the spatial relationships between important hydrological features such as rivers, water bodies, catchments, aquifers and monitoring points.
  • the Australian Solar Climatology Service, which provides satellite-derived spatial and point-locational climatological statistics of global horizontal and direct normal solar data for industry, research, and environmental applications.

The Viewer will include additional NEII conformant environmental data as it becomes available.

The NEII Viewer adopts an open architecture and when a user accesses data is sourced direct from the custodial organisation through a NEII-conformant data service. The NEII Viewer itself does not store data - it provides a map-based view to data stored at-source.

How to use the NEII Viewer

Once on the NEII Viewer, select datasets, then check the box next to a dataset. Clicking on the name of the dataset will zoom the map to the area of the data (or the whole of Australia if it covers the whole country). When a dataset is loaded on the map, you can click on the map to find out more information about that point in the dataset.
